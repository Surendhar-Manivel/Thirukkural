வாழ்க்கை வரலாறு Life & History

Thiruvalluvar is the ancient Tamil poet and philosopher who authored the Thirukkural, one of the greatest works of Tamil literature and world philosophy. His exact dates of birth and death remain unknown — historians place him anywhere between the 4th century BCE and the 5th century CE, with many scholars settling on approximately 300 BCE.

திருவள்ளுவர் பற்றிய சரியான வரலாற்று தகவல்கள் இல்லை. அவர் சென்னையின் மயிலாப்பூரில் வாழ்ந்ததாக நம்பப்படுகிறது. அவரது மனைவி வாசுகி, பதினோரு வயதிலேயே அவரிடம் கற்று ஞானம் பெற்றதாக ஐதீகம்.

He is believed to have lived in Mylapore (present-day Chennai, Tamil Nadu). His wife was Vasuki, described in legend as an extraordinarily virtuous woman who never wasted a drop of water — when Thiruvalluvar placed a needle in a bowl of water and asked her to fill it, she poured water so precisely that not a drop spilled.

திருக்குறள் பற்றி About the Thirukkural

The Thirukkural (literally "Sacred Verses") consists of 1330 couplets organized into 133 chapters of exactly 10 kurals each. Each couplet contains only seven words — four in the first line and three in the second — yet conveys a complete philosophical thought.

The work is divided into three books:

  • அறத்துப்பால் (Aram — Virtue): 380 kurals on ethics, family life, domestic virtue, asceticism, and fate.
  • பொருட்பால் (Porul — Wealth): 700 kurals on governance, statecraft, war, ministers, friendship, and conduct.
  • காமத்துப்பால் (Inbam — Love): 250 kurals on the joy and anguish of romantic love, in the voices of lovers.

The Thirukkural is remarkable for its secular and universal nature — it speaks to all people regardless of religion, caste, or creed. It has been called the "Universal Veda" (உலகப் பொது மறை) and the "Tamil Veda" (தமிழ் வேதம்).

காலவரிசை Historical Timeline

~300 BCE – 500 CE (estimated)
Thiruvalluvar composes the Thirukkural in Mylapore (present-day Chennai).
~7th–8th century CE
Classical Tamil scholars begin writing commentaries (urai) on the Thirukkural. Multiple competing schools of interpretation emerge.
~11th–13th century CE
Parimelalagar writes the most authoritative commentary still used today. He organises the 1330 kurals into the definitive chapter structure.
1730 CE
First European translation into Latin by Costanzo Beschi (Veeramamunivar), introducing Thirukkural to Western scholars.
1856 CE
G.U. Pope publishes a comprehensive English translation, making Thirukkural widely accessible to English-speaking readers worldwide.
1935 CE
Tamil Nadu officially recognises Thiruvalluvar Day. The Thirukkural is declared the state's literary heritage.
2000 CE onwards
The Thirukkural has been translated into over 80 languages. The iconic Thiruvalluvar statue (133 feet tall) is unveiled at Kanyakumari.

நூலின் பெயர்கள் Names of the Work

The Thirukkural is known by many names, each reflecting a different aspect of the work:

முப்பால்
The Three Parts — for its tripartite structure
உலகப் பொது மறை
The Universal Scripture — for its timeless relevance
பொய்யாமொழி
Words That Never Lie — for the truth in each verse
தெய்வநூல்
The Divine Book — for its spiritual authority
